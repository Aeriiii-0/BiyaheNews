Biyahe News

A smart commute planner built for Filipino students who need to get home on a tight budget, in unpredictable weather, and through heavy traffic — every single day.

What this app does

Most commuter apps just tell you how long a route takes. Biyahe News is different — it tells you what you should actually do right now, given your specific situation.

It takes four real factors about your day — your remaining budget, your physical energy level, current weather at your location, and live road congestion — and cross-references them against a decision matrix to produce a single, clear action plan. Not just a route. An actual recommendation: leave now, wait it out, walk around the jam, or spend a little more to get home safe.

The goal is to help you avoid the two most expensive mistakes a commuter student makes: paying surge prices when you're already broke, and pushing through bad conditions when your body can't handle it.

The four inputs — and why they matter

₱ Remaining Budget

Split into three tiers: Low (under ₱100), Normal (₱100–₱250), and High (above ₱250). Budget directly controls whether you can absorb the cost of a ride-hailing app, a direct taxi, or whether you're limited to jeepney fare only. A low budget under bad conditions is the highest-risk scenario — it removes your financial safety net at exactly the moment you might need it most.

⚡ Energy Level

Exhausted, Normal, or Energized. This input exists because physical fatigue changes your risk profile. An exhausted commuter standing in the rain for 45 minutes is at real risk of getting sick or having an accident. Someone energized can walk an extra 15 minutes to bypass a traffic jam without consequence. The app treats exhaustion as a risk multiplier — it makes every other bad condition worse.

🌦 Live Weather

Fetched in real time from OpenWeatherMap using your origin city. The system classifies the sky as Clear or Rainy — where Rainy covers rain, drizzle, and thunderstorms. Rain matters because it does three things at once: it makes walking miserable and dangerous, it causes traffic to slow significantly on Philippine roads due to flooding and poor visibility, and it drives ride-hailing surge prices up sharply.

🚦 Traffic Congestion

Fetched from TomTom Traffic Flow API using the coordinates of your origin city. The system measures the ratio of current road speed to the normal free-flow speed for that area. If traffic is moving at less than 75% of the usual speed, it's classified as High Congestion. If no traffic key is configured, the app falls back to estimating congestion from weather — since rain reliably causes significant slowdowns on Philippine roads, it's treated as a congestion proxy.

How the recommendation is chosen

The four inputs are checked against a priority-ordered set of conditions. The first condition that matches wins.

ConditionBudgetEnergyWeatherTrafficResult
🛑 Stay in Campus Low Exhausted Rain or High or both Wait it out. Commuting now means expensive surges or getting stuck in gridlock while physically depleted.
🚶 Active Bypass Low Energized Clear High Walk past the bottleneck. Traffic is jammed but you have the energy and conditions to bypass it on foot.
🛵 Priority Transit Normal / High Exhausted Rainy Any Spend a bit more. You have the budget buffer — fatigue plus rain is a real safety risk, not worth saving ₱50.
🚌 Green Light Any Normal / Energized Clear Low Take your standard route now. All conditions are stable — this is the ideal window to commute.
